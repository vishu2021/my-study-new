🏛️ 1. POLITY (Topper Style)

Q. Parliamentary privileges are essential for democracy. Discuss.

Introduction

Parliamentary privileges are special rights under Articles 105 & 194, ensuring effective functioning of legislatures in a democracy.

Why Privileges are Essential

👉 Imagine: An MP exposing corruption without fear of legal intimidation.

  • Freedom of Speech in House
    • Enables fearless deliberation
  • Immunity from Legal Action
    • Protects against external pressure
  • Institutional Autonomy
    • Ensures separation from executive/judiciary interference

Critical Concerns

  • Lack of Codification
    • Leads to ambiguity
  • Possibility of Misuse
    • Arbitrary breach of privilege notices
  • Conflict with Fundamental Rights
    • Especially Article 19 (Freedom of Speech)

Way Forward

  • Codification of Privileges
  • Balance with constitutional morality
  • Ensure transparency & accountability

Conclusion

Parliamentary privileges are indispensable for democratic functioning, but must evolve to align with modern constitutional values.

  • Sustainable urban planning
  • Wetland & floodplain restoration
  • Integrated drainage management

Conclusion

Urban flooding is less a natural inevitability and more a result of developmental mismanagement.

“Urban flooding is more man-made than natural. Discuss.”

✅ 🔹 150-WORD VERSION (10 MARKER)

Urban flooding refers to temporary inundation of urban areas due to heavy rainfall combined with inadequate drainage systems. While natural factors like intense monsoon rainfall and climate change-induced extreme events contribute, human activities play a dominant role.

Rapid urbanization has led to concretization, reducing natural infiltration of water. Further, encroachment of floodplains and wetlands has eliminated natural drainage buffers. Poorly designed and clogged drainage systems aggravate waterlogging. Unplanned land-use and weak enforcement of regulations reflect governance failures.

Urban flooding thus represents the interaction of natural hazards with human-induced vulnerabilities. Its impact is disproportionately felt by the urban poor living in informal settlements.

Addressing the issue requires sustainable urban planning, restoration of natural drainage systems, and improved waste management. Therefore, urban flooding is less a natural inevitability and more a consequence of developmental mismanagement.

👉 Word Count: ~148

✅ 🔹 250-WORD VERSION (15 MARKER)

Urban flooding refers to the inundation of urban areas due to excessive rainfall exceeding drainage capacity. While natural factors such as monsoonal variability and climate change-induced extreme weather events contribute, it is largely human activities that intensify the problem.

Rapid and unplanned urbanization has resulted in extensive concretization, which reduces the natural absorption of rainwater. Additionally, encroachment of floodplains, wetlands, and water bodies has disrupted natural drainage channels. Poorly maintained drainage systems, often clogged with solid waste, further aggravate waterlogging.

From a governance perspective, weak implementation of land-use planning and lack of coordination among urban agencies exacerbate the issue. Thus, urban flooding reflects a combination of environmental degradation and institutional failure.

Critically, the impact of urban flooding is uneven, disproportionately affecting vulnerable populations residing in low-lying and informal settlements. It also leads to economic losses, infrastructure damage, and public health risks.

Addressing urban flooding requires a multi-pronged approach, including sustainable urban planning, restoration of wetlands, improved drainage infrastructure, and community awareness. Integration of climate-resilient strategies is also essential.

In conclusion, while natural factors may trigger flooding, it is primarily human actions that convert it into a disaster, making urban flooding largely a man-made phenomenon.
