Q1. How can the mountain ecosystem be restored from the negative impact of development initiatives and tourism? -2019

Mountain ecosystems are characterized by unique biodiversity and ecological processes that are sensitive to human activities.

Development initiatives and tourism have had a significant negative impact on mountain ecosystems, including soil erosion, deforestation, loss of biodiversity, and water pollution.

To restore these ecosystems, the following measures can be taken:

  1. Promoting Sustainable Tourism: Sustainable tourism practices such as eco-tourism, responsible tourism, and community-based tourism can be promoted to reduce the negative impact of tourism on the mountain ecosystem. This includes enforcing regulations on tourism, promoting cultural tourism, and promoting environmental education to tourists.

  2. Reintroducing Native Species: Restoring native plant species in degraded mountain areas can help to stabilize soil, improve biodiversity and water quality, and prevent soil erosion. Restoration can be done through seed dispersal, reforestation, and the reintroduction of species through other means.

  3. Reclamation of Degraded Land: Landslides, soil erosion, and deforestation are some of the significant challenges faced by mountain ecosystems. Restoration of degraded land can be achieved by developing strategies such as terrace farming, contour farming, and the use of soil stabilization methods like vegetation cover.

  4. Sustainable Agriculture Practices: Sustainable agricultural practices such as terracing, mixed cropping, and organic farming can help reduce soil erosion, protect biodiversity, and improve water quality. This also includes reducing the use of chemicals in farming practices, conserving water, and promoting sustainable grazing practices.

  5. Encouraging Sustainable Forestry Practices: Encouraging sustainable forestry practices like agroforestry, promoting community-based forestry, and increasing the use of non-timber forest products can help protect biodiversity, improve water quality, and promote sustainable livelihoods.

Recent examples of efforts to restore mountain ecosystems include:

  1. The Himalayan Afforestation Project: This project was launched in 2015 in Uttarakhand to combat climate change and restore the degraded forests of the Himalayas. It aims to plant 2.5 billion trees by 2030 to help restore the ecological balance of the region.

  2. The National Mission for Sustaining the Himalayan Ecosystem: Launched in 2015, this mission focuses on conserving and restoring the Himalayan ecosystem by developing sustainable livelihoods, promoting sustainable tourism, and enhancing biodiversity.

  3. The Zero Budget Natural Farming Project: This project, launched in Himachal Pradesh in 2019, aims to promote sustainable agriculture practices such as zero-budget natural farming to improve soil fertility, reduce soil erosion, and promote biodiversity conservation.

In conclusion, restoring mountain ecosystems is critical for the sustainable development of mountain regions. Promoting sustainable tourism, reintroducing native species, reclaiming degraded land, promoting sustainable agriculture and forestry practices, and creating awareness about the importance of ecosystem conservation are some of the measures that can help to achieve this goal.

Q2. “The Himalayas are highly prone to landslides.” Discuss the causes and suggest suitable measures of mitigation. - 2016

The Himalayan region is one of the most landslide-prone regions in the world. Landslides are a natural process in this region but the rapid pace of development, along with climate change, has increased their frequency and severity.

In this context, it is important to understand the causes of landslides and suggest suitable measures of mitigation.

Causes of Landslides:

  1. Geological factors: The Himalayan region is geologically unstable due to the presence of young and active mountains. The rocks in the region are highly fractured and weathered, making them more susceptible to landslides.

  2. Steep slopes: The Himalayan region is characterized by steep slopes, which increase the potential for landslides.

  3. Heavy rainfall: The region receives heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, which saturates the soil and increases the likelihood of landslides.

  4. Earthquakes: The Himalayan region is highly seismically active, and earthquakes can trigger landslides by destabilizing the slopes.

Mitigation Measures:

  1. Early warning systems: It is important to have an early warning system in place to predict landslides and alert people in the affected areas. The use of technology such as GPS, satellite imagery, and sensors can help in early detection and warning of landslides.

  2. Land-use planning: The government should enforce strict land-use planning regulations to prevent construction in landslide-prone areas.
