Q1. “If the last few decades were of Asia’s growth story, the next few are expected to be of Africa’s.”

In the light of this statement, examine India’s influence in Africa in recent years. - 2021

In recent years, India has been trying to enhance its presence in Africa and strengthen its ties with African nations.

This can be attributed to the growing importance of Africa in global affairs and the opportunities it offers for economic cooperation and regional stability.

Some of the ways in which India has been increasing its influence in Africa are:

  1. Trade and Investment: India has been increasing its economic engagement with Africa through trade and investment. In 2019-20, India's trade with Africa stood at $62.67 billion. India has also been investing in various sectors in Africa, such as agriculture, health, education, infrastructure, and energy. The India-Africa Business Forum, which was launched in 2015, has been playing a key role in promoting business partnerships between Indian and African companies.

  2. Development Assistance: India has been providing development assistance to African countries through its flagship programs such as the Pan African e-Network, India-Africa Forum Summit, and Lines of Credit. India has also been providing technical assistance and capacity building to African countries in various sectors such as healthcare, education, agriculture, and information technology.

  3. Defense and Security Cooperation: India has been strengthening its defense and security ties with African nations. India has signed defense cooperation agreements with several African countries, including South Africa, Tanzania, and Seychelles. India has also been providing training and equipment to African countries to enhance their defense capabilities.

  4. Diplomacy: India has been increasing its diplomatic engagement with African countries. India has opened several embassies and consulates in Africa in recent years. India has also been participating in various forums and summits such as the India-Africa Forum Summit and the India-Africa Business Forum to strengthen its ties with African nations.

However, despite India's efforts to enhance its influence in Africa, there are several challenges that need to be addressed.

These challenges include:

  1. Competition from other countries: India faces competition from other countries such as China, the US, and Russia, which have been increasing their presence in Africa in recent years.

  2. Lack of infrastructure: Many African countries lack the necessary infrastructure to support economic growth and development. This poses a challenge to India's efforts to enhance economic cooperation with African nations.

  3. Political instability and conflict: Many African countries are affected by political instability and conflict, which can impact India's engagement with these nations.

  4. Limited resources: India's limited resources pose a challenge to its efforts to enhance its presence in Africa. India needs to prioritize its engagement with African countries to maximize its impact.

In conclusion, India's influence in Africa has been growing in recent years, and India has been trying to enhance its engagement with African nations through various means. However, India needs to address the challenges it faces in Africa to further strengthen its ties with African nations.

Q2. “The USA is facing an existential threat in the form of a China, that is much more challenging than the erstwhile Soviet Union.”

Explain. - 2021

The relationship between the United States and China has become increasingly tense over the past few years, with many experts characterizing it as the most significant geopolitical challenge of our time.

The following are some of the reasons why the United States is facing an existential threat in the form of China:

  1. Economic Competition: China has emerged as a significant economic competitor to the United States, and its growing influence threatens American economic dominance. Over the last few years, China has been able to expand its economic influence in many parts of the world, particularly in Asia and Africa, challenging the traditional US-led global economic order. China’s Belt and Road Initiative, for example, is seen as a significant threat to US economic interests.

  2. Military and Technological Competition: China has also been investing heavily in modernizing its military and technological capabilities. China has been making rapid progress in areas such as artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and 5G technology, which have significant strategic and economic implications. China's military modernization and territorial ambitions in the South China Sea, East China Sea, and Indian Ocean have also raised alarm bells in Washington.

  3. Ideological and Value-based Competition: There is a growing sense in the United States that China's rise represents an existential threat to the American way of life. China's authoritarian political system, lack of individual freedoms, and human rights violations have led many in the US to view China as a threat to the liberal, democratic, and capitalist values that the US represents.

  4. Diplomatic Competition: The United States and China have been engaged in a diplomatic competition for influence in many parts of the world. China has been increasingly using its economic and diplomatic clout to challenge US influence in Asia and Africa, which has led to a competition for influence in the region.

Recent Examples:

  1. The US-China Trade War: In 2018, the United States initiated a trade war with China, imposing tariffs on Chinese goods worth billions of dollars. The trade war led to a significant escalation in tensions between the two countries and highlighted the growing economic competition between the US and China.

  2. South China Sea Dispute: China's territorial ambitions in the South China Sea have led to increased tensions between the US and China. The US has been conducting freedom of navigation operations in the South China Sea, challenging China's claims to the disputed waters.

  3. Hong Kong Protests: The recent protests in Hong Kong, which were triggered by China's attempts to impose a national security law on the city, have raised concerns about China's growing authoritarianism and the erosion of Hong Kong's autonomy. The US has been critical of China's actions in Hong Kong, which has further escalated tensions between the two countries.

  4. Technology Competition: The US has been taking steps to limit China's technological advancements by imposing restrictions on Chinese companies such as Huawei, which has been accused of facilitating Chinese espionage and posing a national security risk to the US.

In conclusion, the United States is facing an existential threat from China due to its growing economic, military, and technological capabilities, as well as its challenge to the liberal democratic values that the US represents. The US-China competition is likely to shape the geopolitical landscape for the next few decades, and managing this competition will be a significant challenge for the US.

Q3. Critically examine the aims and objectives of SCO. What importance does it hold for India? - 2021

The Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) is a regional intergovernmental organisation founded in 2001 by China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. India and Pakistan were admitted as full members in 2017, expanding the organisation's reach and influence.

The aims and objectives of the SCO can be examined as follows:

Aims and Objectives:

  1. To promote cooperation in economic, trade, and investment fields.

  2. To enhance regional security and stability.

  3. To strengthen cultural and people-to-people exchanges.

  4. To combat terrorism, separatism, and extremism.

  5. To facilitate cooperation in science, technology, and innovation.

  6. To establish dialogue and consultation between member states.

Importance for India:

  1. Economic Benefits: The SCO provides India with a platform to expand economic cooperation with member states. It offers opportunities for India to tap into Central Asian markets, especially in sectors such as energy, agriculture, and infrastructure development.

  2. Counter-terrorism Cooperation: India, which has been a victim of terrorism, can benefit from the SCO's efforts to combat terrorism, separatism, and extremism. It can also use the platform to build closer counter-terrorism cooperation with member states, especially China and Russia.

  3. Regional Security: The SCO can play a significant role in enhancing regional security, stability, and cooperation in South Asia. India can use the forum to engage with member states on issues such as border disputes, cross-border terrorism, and drug trafficking.

  4. Multilateralism: India's participation in the SCO demonstrates its commitment to multilateralism, regional cooperation, and the promotion of a rules-based international order.

Recent Examples:

  1. In September 2021, India's External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar attended the SCO foreign ministers' meeting in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. He discussed various issues such as Afghanistan, terrorism, and regional security with his counterparts from member states.

  2. In August 2021, India participated in the SCO joint anti-terrorism exercise in Russia. The exercise was aimed at improving the coordination and cooperation between member states in dealing with terrorist threats.

  3. In June 2021, India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi attended the SCO summit hosted by Tajikistan. He emphasised the importance of regional security, economic cooperation, and cultural exchanges among member states.

  4. In April 2021, India and China held their first-ever joint military exercise under the aegis of the SCO. The exercise aimed to enhance mutual trust and cooperation between the two countries' armies.

  5. In November 2020, India hosted the SCO Council of Heads of Government meeting virtually. The meeting focused on economic cooperation and regional connectivity among member states.

  6. In conclusion, the SCO provides India with a valuable platform to enhance regional cooperation, economic ties, and counter-terrorism efforts. India can leverage the organisation to promote its strategic interests in the region and strengthen its ties with member states.

Q4. The newly tri-nation partnership AUKUS is aimed at countering China’s ambitions in the Indo-Pacific region.

Is it going to supersede the existing partnerships in the region?

Discuss the strength and impact of AUKUS in the present scenario. - 2021

AUKUS, the newly formed trilateral partnership between Australia, the United Kingdom, and the United States, has been established to counter China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific region. It was announced in September 2021, and the partnership aims to provide Australia with nuclear-powered submarines and advanced military technology.

This partnership has been viewed as a significant step in the US pivot towards the Indo-Pacific region and a boost to regional security.

Strengths and Impact of AUKUS:

  1. Military Strength: One of the primary strengths of AUKUS is the military capabilities that the three nations bring to the table. The United States and the United Kingdom are two of the world's most powerful military powers, and Australia is a key regional player. The three nations possess advanced military technology, including nuclear-powered submarines, which will enhance their ability to counter China's military expansion in the region.

  2. Economic Impact: AUKUS is expected to have a significant economic impact on the region. The partnership will lead to increased economic cooperation and investment between the three nations, which could spur economic growth and job creation. The focus on security in the region could also encourage more countries to invest in the region.

  3. Superseding Existing Partnerships: AUKUS is not expected to supersede existing partnerships in the region, such as the Quad (comprising India, Japan, Australia, and the United States) or ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations). Rather, it is expected to complement these partnerships, allowing for more effective cooperation and coordination in the region.

  4. Impact on China: AUKUS has caused a significant backlash from China, which views the partnership as an attempt to contain its rise as a global power. The partnership has further intensified tensions between the United States and China, which were already strained due to trade disputes and geopolitical tensions.

  5. Impact on India: India is not a part of AUKUS, but it could have significant implications for India's security and strategic interests. India has been working to strengthen its ties with Australia and the United States through the Quad partnership, and the emergence of AUKUS could provide further opportunities for cooperation.

In conclusion, AUKUS is a significant development in the Indo-Pacific region that aims to counter China's growing influence. While it has significant military and economic potential, it is not expected to supersede existing partnerships in the region. Its impact on China and India's strategic interests is yet to be fully understood, but it is likely to intensify tensions in the region.

Q5. What is the significance of Indo-US defence deals over Indo-Russian defence deals?

Discuss with reference to stability in the Indo-Pacific region. - 2020

Indo-US defence deals and Indo-Russian defence deals have been instrumental in shaping the defence scenario in the Indo-Pacific region. While Indo-Russian defence deals have a long-standing history, the emergence of Indo-US defence deals in recent years has significantly impacted the region's stability.

Significance of Indo-US defence deals:

  1. Strategic partnership: Indo-US defence deals have resulted in a strategic partnership between the two countries. The US has emerged as one of the largest defence suppliers to India, and the two countries have collaborated on several projects, including defence technology and intelligence sharing.

  2. Countering China's rise: The Indo-US defence deals are aimed at countering China's growing military assertiveness in the region. India and the US have a shared interest in maintaining the freedom of navigation in the Indo-Pacific, and the defence deals are an important component of this objective.

  3. Access to advanced technology: The Indo-US defence deals have provided India access to advanced defence technology and equipment, which has helped modernize its armed forces. This has also led to the transfer of technology to India, which will help the country in developing its own defence industry.

Significance of Indo-Russian defence deals:

  1. Long-standing relationship: India and Russia have a long-standing relationship in the defence sector, with Russia being one of India's largest defence suppliers. The two countries have collaborated on several projects, including the development of the BrahMos missile and the Fifth Generation Fighter Aircraft.

  2. Diversification of defence supplies: The Indo-Russian defence deals have helped India diversify its defence supplies and reduce its dependence on any single country. This has also helped India in negotiating better prices and terms for its defence purchases.

  3. Strategic autonomy: The Indo-Russian defence deals have helped India maintain its strategic autonomy and avoid getting caught up in any power bloc. India has always pursued an independent foreign policy, and the defence deals with Russia have helped it maintain this stance.

Impact on the stability in the Indo-Pacific region:

  1. Indo-US defence deals have caused concerns among other countries in the region, especially China, which sees it as an attempt to contain its rise. This has led to a more assertive China, which has increased tensions in the region.

  2. The Indo-Russian defence deals have helped maintain a balance of power in the region, which has contributed to its stability. India's partnership with Russia has helped it maintain its strategic autonomy and avoid getting caught up in any power bloc, which has been a stabilizing factor in the region.

  3. The significance of Indo-US defence deals and Indo-Russian defence deals for the stability in the Indo-Pacific region cannot be compared as they serve different purposes. While Indo-US defence deals are aimed at countering China's rise, Indo-Russian defence deals have helped India diversify its defence supplies and maintain its strategic autonomy.

In conclusion, while Indo-US defence deals have been instrumental in countering China's rise in the region, Indo-Russian defence deals have helped India diversify its defence supplies and maintain its strategic autonomy. Both these partnerships have their significance in the region's stability, and India will need to balance its engagement with both countries to ensure its national security interests.

Q6. ‘Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (QUAD)’ is the transforming itself into a trade bloc from a military alliance, in present times – Discuss. - 2020

The Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (QUAD), comprising India, Japan, Australia, and the United States, was initially conceived as a military alliance to balance China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific region. However, in recent times, there has been a shift towards transforming the QUAD into a trade bloc.

Here are some points that explain the shift in QUAD's objectives:

  1. Economic cooperation: With China's aggressive economic expansion, the QUAD countries have realized the importance of cooperating on trade and economic issues. They have been working to establish supply chain resilience in critical sectors such as technology, healthcare, and infrastructure. The COVID-19 pandemic has further emphasized the need for diversifying supply chains and reducing dependence on China.

  2. China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI): China's BRI is a massive infrastructure development project that seeks to connect Asia, Africa, and Europe through a network of highways, railways, ports, and other infrastructure projects. The BRI has raised concerns about debt-trap diplomacy and environmental degradation, leading to the QUAD countries developing their own infrastructure development projects.

The World Bank and the International Monetary Fund (IMF) are two inter-governmental institutions established at the Bretton Woods Conference in 1944.

While these two organizations have similarities in their structure and functions, they have distinct roles, functions, and mandates.

Role and Mandate:

  1. The World Bank's primary mandate is to provide loans, credits, and grants to developing countries to support their economic and social development. Its focus is on poverty reduction, sustainable economic growth, and the promotion of shared prosperity.

  2. The IMF, on the other hand, has a mandate to promote international monetary cooperation and exchange rate stability, facilitate balanced international trade, and provide resources to help countries in need to meet balance of payment problems.

Membership and Governance:

  1. The World Bank has 189 member countries, each with a proportional number of voting shares based on their economic contributions to the institution. The World Bank's Board of Governors is the highest decision-making body of the institution, and the World Bank President serves as its chair.

  2. The IMF has 190 member countries, with the same proportional voting share system as the World Bank. The IMF's highest decision-making body is its Board of Governors, which meets annually, and the IMF Managing Director chairs the Executive Board, which is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the institution.

Loan Programs:

  1. The World Bank provides loans and credits for long-term development projects, such as infrastructure, education, health, and agriculture. It also provides technical assistance to support developing countries in implementing development policies and programs.

  2. The IMF provides short-term loans, called Stand-By Arrangements, to countries facing balance of payment problems. It also provides policy advice and technical assistance to help countries achieve macroeconomic stability.

Conditionality:

  1. The World Bank's loans are generally conditioned on the borrower's implementation of specific development policies and programs aimed at reducing poverty and promoting sustainable economic growth.

  2. The IMF's loans are often subject to macroeconomic policy conditionality, which requires countries to implement specific economic policies, such as fiscal and monetary policies, aimed at restoring macroeconomic stability.
